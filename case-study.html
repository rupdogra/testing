AI-Powered CRM System

Role

Lead Product Designer

Timeline

6 months

Team

Design, Engineering, Product

Tools

Figma, Framer, User Testing

The Challenge

Enterprise CRM systems are notoriously complex. Users spend more time navigating the interface than actually managing relationships. The existing system had poor information architecture, inconsistent workflows, and high cognitive load—leading to low adoption rates and frustrated teams.

Our goal: Redesign the entire CRM workflow to reduce friction, improve task completion rates, and make the system intuitive enough that users prefer it to spreadsheets and email.

Objective

  • Reduce task completion time by 40%
  • Improve user satisfaction scores from 3.2 to 4.5+
  • Increase feature adoption from 30% to 80%
  • Create a scalable design system for future iterations

Process

Research & Discovery

We conducted 15 user interviews, analyzed usage patterns, and identified pain points. Key finding: users spent 60% of their time searching for information instead of taking action. This insight drove our entire redesign strategy.

Information Architecture

We restructured the navigation based on user mental models, not organizational hierarchy. Instead of organizing by features, we organized by user workflows: "Manage Relationships," "Track Deals," "Collaborate," and "Analyze."

Behavioral Design

Applied behavioral psychology principles to reduce decision fatigue. Implemented smart defaults, progressive disclosure, and contextual help. Every interaction was designed to guide users toward the right action.

Validation & Iteration

Tested prototypes with 40+ users across different roles. Iterated based on feedback, focusing on the highest-impact workflows first. Achieved 92% task completion in final testing vs. 45% in the original system.

Key Learnings

  • Understand the workflow, not just the feature: The best designs solve the user's actual problem, not the feature they asked for.
  • Behavioral design beats visual design: How the system guides users matters more than how it looks.
  • Test with real workflows: Lab testing showed 92% completion, but real-world usage revealed edge cases that required iteration.
  • Scalability from day one: Building a design system early saved months of rework during the enterprise rollout.
